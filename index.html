  • The Success Equation
    I have always had big dreams. That inner sense that something about me was special. Like I was going to be someone that mattered in the world. This delusion starkly contradicts everything I believe in. I don’t believe in destiny or fate or that things happen for a reason. I think that your life is determined by two things: the actions you take, and the natural randomness of the universe or what some people like to call luck. Some people think they are successful because some external force is supporting them. Like they are some sort of chosen one. A messiah. A prophet. Others attribute their success to pure hard work and resilience. If you try to convince them that luck is involved they would deny it and make snarky remarks about how luck is a way people make themselves feel better about their own failures. Denying the existence of luck or as I like to call it RNG (short for randomly generated number a more relatable term to the computer age we live in) to me is more bizarre than denying the existence of god. If you give me 5 minutes and a piece of paper I can probably list 10 clear pieces of evidence that support the existence of RNG. In contrast if you give a theist a millenia and all the paper that can be made if we cut the entire Amazon rainforest they would still have a hard time convincing somebody that god actually exists let alone give a single clear piece of evidence. Every single time a cell divides you are playing russian roulette on whether that cell becomes cancerous or not and whether your own immune system which you have zero control over is able to recognize the danger of that cancerous cell or not. At any random moment in time any random cell can become a malignant cell and if your immune system fails to recognize the danger of that cell you are likely to develop cancer. Every time you step into your car you are at risk of getting into a fatal accident. These are what I like to call terminal RNG. It is the type of bad RNG that you have no control over. You could waste your life worrying about this type of RNG and it will ruin your life. On the other hand there is a type of RNG that is less terminal. A type that you can actually fight back against. For example, being born poor, having abusive parents, developing a chronic disease like diabetes. These types of bad RNG will still make your life harder but they don’t make it impossible to succeed. It is often these types of RNG that successful people quote having to tell people that they weren’t lucky. The football star forgets that he could have been born without feet. The gorgeous actress forgets that hard work didn’t give her that beautiful face. No matter how much work you do, if you don’t have the right RNG, it is impossible to be successful. The success equation = RNG x Work. Now this whole time I was trying to convince you that you have no control over your RNG but that is not true. In fact RNG is not a single constant for every area of your life. You might be ugly but intelligent. You might lack athletic abilities but have an artistic mind. This variability is very important because if you play to your strength you will maximize your chances of success. In order to get the highest chance of success one must focus on maximizing both sides of the equation. If you get stuck spending your time and energy on something that you lack the natural ability to do, your chances of success will still be low despite your hard work. You might end up being one of those people calling other successful people lucky and denying that hard work matters. Both this type of person and the type I described as denying that luck matters are wrong. Because they are both part of the success equation and if you neglect either of them you are doing yourself a disservice and decreasing your chances of success dramatically.
